Specialized Leak Detection Services

Acoustic Leak Detection

  • Utilizes specialized listening equipment to detect leaks via sound waves.
  • Ideal for locating leaks in pressurized water pipes.

Infrared Thermography

  • Employs thermal imaging to find temperature changes caused by leaking water.
  • Perfect for identifying hidden leaks behind walls and under floors.

Video Camera Pipe Inspection

  • Involves using a waterproof camera to inspect the interior of plumbing systems.
  • Effective for pinpointing the exact location of leaks in complex piping networks.

Frequently Asked Questions

Leak Detection and Repair (LDAR) is a program or process implemented by industries to identify and repair leaks of hazardous substances, such as gases or liquids, to prevent emissions and enhance safety.

LDAR is crucial because it helps in minimizing the environmental impact of industrial processes by reducing pollutant emissions. It also improves safety, regulatory compliance, and can provide cost savings by preventing the loss of valuable resources.

The frequency of leak detection depends on regulatory requirements, the type of facility, and the substances being handled. However, routine inspections are often conducted on a quarterly basis, with more frequent checks in high-risk areas.

Common leak detection methods include ultrasonic leak detectors, infrared cameras, soap bubble tests, and electronic leak detectors. Each method has its specific applications depending on the type of leaks and accessibility.

Once a leak is detected, it should be documented, prioritized based on its size and potential impact, and then repaired. Follow-up checks should be conducted to ensure the effectiveness of the repair and compliance with safety and environmental regulations.
