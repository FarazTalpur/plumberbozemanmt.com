Frequently Asked Questions

Common signs include strange noises, frequent clogs, leaks, foul odors, and the disposal not turning on or frequently resetting.

If you are comfortable with basic plumbing and electrical work, you can install a garbage disposal yourself. However, if you're unsure, it's best to hire a professional to ensure proper installation and avoid potential issues.

It's recommended to clean your garbage disposal every one to two weeks to prevent buildup of debris and odors, using ice cubes, citrus peels, or a baking soda and vinegar mixture.

Avoid putting grease, oils, fats, fibrous foods like celery or corn husks, large bones, egg shells, coffee grounds, and non-food items down the disposal, as these can cause clogs and damage.

First, turn off the disposal and unplug it. Use the provided wrench or a hex key to manually turn the motor and dislodge the jam. After clearing the jam, reset the disposal and test it to ensure it's operating properly.
